## Design Context

### Users
- People seeking psychological services: therapy, educational psychology assessments, vocational counseling
- Potential clients researching a practitioner before making contact
- Returning visitors reading monthly blog content for insights and self-help
- Context: They are often in a vulnerable or exploratory state, looking for someone they can trust

### Brand Personality
- **3 words**: Trustworthy, Calm, Inspirational
- **Voice**: Warm but professional. Grounded and approachable, never clinical or cold. Speaks with quiet confidence.
- **Emotional goals**: Visitors should feel safe, understood, and gently motivated

### Professional Identity
- Clinical Psychologist
- Educational Psychologist  
- Vocational Counselor

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Serious and creative, yet down-to-earth and approachable
- **Theme**: Light mode primary (calming), with considered use of warm, natural tones
- **Anti-references**: Generic therapy websites with stock photos of sunsets/hands. No corporate SaaS aesthetics. No AI-generated "wellness" cliches.
- **Feel in first 3 seconds**: "This person is thoughtful, competent, and someone I could talk to"

### Design Principles
1. **Calm Authority** - Every element should communicate competence without intimidation
2. **Intentional Warmth** - Approachability through typography, color, and spacing choices, not through decoration
3. **Content-First** - The blog and services are the substance; design serves readability and trust
4. **Distinctive Restraint** - Stand out through craft and taste, not through flashiness
5. **Accessible by Default** - WCAG AA minimum, considerate of users in emotional distress

### Primary Actions
1. **Get in touch** - Contact form / booking CTA, always reachable
2. **Read blog content** - Monthly articles, easy to browse and read
